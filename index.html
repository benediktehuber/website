Research Stream

Research Focus: Benedikte Grundtvig Huber's research explores contemporary democracy with a particular focus on democratic justice, mutual understanding, and post-coup regimes. She aims to enhance democratic resilience by combining experimental methods with historical and contemporary case analysis.

Current Workstreams:

  • Democratic Justice among Danish Citizens and Politicians
  • Examining Political Polarization and the Rise of the Radical Right
  • Fostering Peaceful Post-Coup Regimes
  • Studying Democratic Recession

Fieldwork: Benedikte has conducted fieldwork in Appalachia, aiming to understand why economically disadvantaged voters support Donald Trump.

Recent Readings: Benedikte is currently reading various works related to political protests, repression, and the dynamics of contentious action, including texts by Lichbach, Goldstone, and Della Porta.

Ongoing Projects: Benedikte is engaged in a series of papers on the impact of economic factors on voting behavior and how they relate to political mobilization.
